Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need to find a vehicle to offload the dumpster after it's delivered?

No, you don’t need to do that. Our service covers everything from delivery to pickup. Just fill the dumpster, and we’ll handle the rest.

How do you calculate the final weight of my debris?

All of our trucks are required to go to a certified weigh station at the disposal facility. They weigh the truck when it's full (gross weight) and again after it's been emptied (tare weight). The difference between these two numbers is the exact, certified weight of your disposed material, a copy of which is available to you.

What is a "trip fee" and how do I avoid it?

A trip fee is a charge incurred if our driver arrives for a scheduled delivery or pickup and cannot complete the service due to an obstacle, like a parked car blocking the placement area. You can avoid it by ensuring the entire access path is clear and unobstructed on your service day.

What do you consider a long-term rental period?

We consider a 28-day rental period as our standard for long-term dumpster rentals. However, we’re open to flexible rental extensions. If you need more time, just let us know!

I have a lot of scrap metal. Do you pay for it or is there still a hauling fee?

While we do not purchase scrap metal directly, we can provide a dedicated roll off container for it. Depending on the volume and current market rates for metal, we can often offer a significantly reduced hauling fee or, in some cases, a no-cost haul for clean-loaded, high-value scrap. Please call us to discuss your specific material.

How much do you charge for long-term rental periods?

For our standard long-term rental period, we charge between $300 and $1,500 depending on the size of the dumpster and your location.

Anderson Island, WA Insights: Population,
Zip Codes, Influence, and Service Areas

Anderson Island is the southernmost island in Puget Sound and a census-designated place of Pierce County, Washington, United States. It is accessible by boat or a 20-minute ferry ride from Steilacoom. Anderson Island is just south of McNeil Island. To the northwest, Key Peninsula is across Drayton Passage. The south basin of Puget Sound separates the island from the mainland to the southeast, while to the southwest the Nisqually Reach of Puget Sound separates the island from the mainland.
